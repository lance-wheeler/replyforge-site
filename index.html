How it works

Four phases. Forty-five days to predictable pipeline.

We don't blast volume. We build infrastructure. The first 14 days are setup. Weeks 3–6 we ramp. From month 2 onward, your calendar fills like clockwork.

01
Days 1–3
Discovery & ICP

60-min kickoff call. We extract your ideal customer profile, value prop, objection patterns, and target outcomes. You get a written ICP brief and campaign strategy doc within 48 hours of signing.

02
Days 3–10
Infrastructure

We provision 3–10 sending domains and 12–40 inboxes. SPF, DKIM, DMARC configured. Native warmup runs for 7–10 days. Your primary brand domain is never used for cold sends — it stays clean.

03
Days 10–14
Copy & Targeting

We write your 5–7 step sequence using a Claude-powered prompt library tested across hundreds of campaigns. Lists are pulled from Apollo + verified at 98%+ accuracy. You approve everything before we send a single email.

04
Day 14+
Launch & Optimize

Campaigns go live. Daily volume ramps from 20 to 40 emails per inbox. Replies are auto-categorized by AI and routed to your calendar. Weekly reports. Bi-weekly A/B tests. You see meetings on your calendar by week 4.

A meeting is qualified when (a) the prospect replied positively, (b) confirmed a calendar slot, (c) is a decision-maker or has stated influence over the buying decision, and (d) attended or rescheduled. No-shows that reschedule within 14 days still count. Pure no-shows are replaced by additional bookings, free of charge. The full definition is written into your service agreement so there's no ambiguity.
Yes — for B2B, in most jurisdictions. CAN-SPAM (US) permits opt-out-based commercial email. GDPR (EU) permits B2B outreach under legitimate interest with required disclosures. CASL (Canada) is stricter; we exclude Canadian addresses by default unless you specifically request and document implied consent. Every email we send includes a compliant footer with sender identity, physical address, and one-click opt-out. We process unsubscribes within 24 hours, well inside the 10-business-day legal requirement.
